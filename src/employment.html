Employment

We are currently seeking applicants for two positions beginning in November 2016 and one immediately:

Hostel Manager

We are accepting applicants to begin in November 2016 to help manage the hostel. You must be able to commit to a minimum of fourteen months, with a preference for longer commitments. You must speak a advanced Spanish and be fluent in English. If you speak multiple languages, this is even better!

You should have an interest in eco-tourism, great people skills, and be willing to work ten+ hour days. We will give preference to applicants who have traveled extensively in Central America, understand hostel life, are comfortable living in the campo, and have management and/or hospitality experience. Surfers are encouraged to apply.

Your responsibilities will include day-to-day operations of running the hostel, including managing staff. You will be paid a monthly salary plus a share in the profits, and on-site room and board are provided.

Surf and Yoga Instructor

We are actively searching for a surf instructor who is also certified to teach yoga to begin immediately.

As a surf instructor you will be responsible for renting boards to guests and reviewing boards for damages, providing beginner and intermediate surf lessons and tips, and guiding guests on off-shore surf trips.

As a yoga instructor, you must be certified and willing to provide a minimum of four classes per week.

We request a minimum time commitment of two months, with a preference given to those who can commit to a longer term. You must speak advanced English and preferably speak Spanish well enough to provide surf lessons in Spanish. You should have experience living abroad in a rural setting, and have experience in hostel-life. Room and board or commissions on classes will be discussed.

Please get in touch with us at stay@rancho-esperanza.com if you are interested in either of these positions, or if you have any questions about them.

Program Assistant

We are currently accepting applicants to begin in November 2016 to help manage our community programs.

You must be able to commit to a minimum of six months, with a preference for a longer commitment.. You must speak intermediate/advanced Spanish. Your main job is working in our afterschool program daily, and coordinating volunteers working in all programs. Therefore, you should have experience working with children abroad, planning and coordinating fun and engaging activities for children ages 5-14, and managing volunteers. You will be assisting our local manager in coordinating our community programs and maintaining community relations. Previous experience working with an international NGO, and ease of living in the campo is helpful. A room and board and visa expenses are provided.

Please get in touch with us at shana@puentes-de-esperanza.org if you meet the qualifications and are seriously interested in this position, or if you have any questions about it.
